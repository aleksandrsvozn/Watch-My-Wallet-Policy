Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 2026-02-13 • Last Updated: 2026-02-24

Wally, officially known as Watch My Wallet ("App", "we", "us", "our") is operated by Aleksandrs Vozņarskis. We respect your privacy and are committed to transparency about how we collect, use, and protect your information. This Privacy Policy applies to your use of the App on Android and iOS devices.

By using the App, you agree to the practices described in this Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

1. Who We Are (Data Controller)

For the purposes of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and other applicable privacy laws, the data controller is:

Aleksandrs Vozņarskis
Email: support@watch-my-wallet.com
Country: Latvia, European Union

2. What Data We Collect

2.1 Data You Provide Directly

  • Account data: your Google account email address and Google user identifier, collected when you sign in via Firebase Authentication.
  • Profile data: names you assign to financial profiles within the App.
  • Financial data: transactions, categories, wallets, balances, recurring rules, and all other records you manually enter into the App.
  • App settings: your preferences such as display language, app theme, and default currency.

2.2 Data Generated Automatically

  • Anonymous device identifier: the App generates and stores a persistent anonymous identifier ("device ID") on your device. This identifier is used solely to associate your local data with sync operations and does not identify you personally. It is not linked to your name, email, or any other directly identifying information.
  • Sync metadata: timestamps, version counters, and conflict-resolution data required to synchronise your data across devices.
  • Analytics data (Android only): the App collects anonymised usage events via Firebase Analytics to understand how users interact with the App. This includes screen views, button taps, and feature usage. Financial amounts are never sent — only anonymised ranges (e.g., "0–10", "10–50"). Custom category names are replaced with "custom" before transmission. Firebase assigns an anonymous app-instance identifier (a pseudonymous identifier under GDPR) to your device installation. No analytics data is collected on iOS.

2.3 Data We Do Not Collect

  • We do not collect payment card numbers or banking credentials.
  • We do not access any Google Drive files other than the App's own backup folder (appDataFolder).
  • We do not collect your precise location.
  • We do not collect biometric data.

4. How We Use Your Data

We use your data to:

  • Provide, operate, and maintain the App and its features
  • Authenticate your identity and manage your account
  • Synchronise your data across devices via Google Drive (if enabled)
  • Process subscription payments and verify subscription status
  • Respond to your support requests
  • Understand how the App is used through anonymised analytics (Android only) to improve features and user experience
  • Comply with our legal obligations

5. Google Drive Backup

If you enable sync, the App:

  • Uses Google Sign-In to obtain your consent and access credentials
  • Requests only the drive.appdata scope — the minimal permission that allows read/write access exclusively to the App's private appDataFolder in your Google Drive
  • Does not access, read, or modify any other files or folders in your Google Drive
  • Encrypts backup data on-device before uploading, using a passphrase you create — your data is end-to-end encrypted and we cannot read it

Important: You are solely responsible for remembering your encryption passphrase. If you lose your passphrase, your encrypted backup cannot be recovered by us or by Google.

Service Purpose Data Processed
Firebase Authentication Account sign-in and identity management Email address, Google user ID, authentication tokens

If additional Firebase services are introduced in the future (such as Cloud Firestore for subscription status storage, Firebase Crashlytics, or others), this Policy will be updated accordingly.

Data processed by Firebase is subject to Google's Privacy Policy and Google's Data Processing Terms. Firebase data may be stored and processed in the United States and other countries where Google operates.

7. Currency Exchange Rates (Frankfurter API)

The App retrieves currency exchange rate data from the Frankfurter API (frankfurter.app), a third-party, open-source service. When you use currency conversion features, the App makes requests to this service. These requests do not include your personal financial data — only a request for exchange rate data.

Exchange rates are cached on-device for up to 24 hours. We are not affiliated with Frankfurter, and we have no control over the accuracy, availability, or data practices of that service. Exchange rates are provided for informational convenience only and must not be relied upon for financial transactions or reporting.

8. Subscriptions and Billing

Note: (This section applies when subscription features are made available.)

Subscription payments are processed by the platform through which you obtained the App:

  • Android (Google Play): payments and subscription management are handled by Google LLC. Google processes your billing information in accordance with its own privacy policy. We receive subscription status (active/expired) and a subscription identifier from Google, but we do not receive your payment card details.
  • iOS (App Store): payments and subscription management are handled by Apple Inc. in accordance with Apple's privacy policy. We receive subscription status and a transaction identifier.

Subscription status may be stored using Firebase services to allow the App to verify your entitlement across devices.

9. Data Sharing and Third Parties

9.1 What We Do Not Do

  • We do not sell, rent, or trade your personally identifiable financial data to third parties.

9.2 Service Providers

We share data with third-party service providers only to the extent necessary to operate the App. Current providers include Google LLC (Firebase, Google Drive, Google Play). These providers act as data processors on our behalf and are contractually bound to process data only as instructed and to implement appropriate security measures.

9.3 Aggregated and Anonymised Data

We may share or license Aggregated Data — information that has been aggregated across users and de-identified so that it cannot reasonably be used to identify any individual — with third parties, including for commercial purposes. For example, this may include anonymised spending category trends, usage statistics, or general behavioural patterns. Aggregated Data does not constitute personal data under GDPR and may be used and disclosed without restriction.

9.4 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your data if required to do so by law, court order, or governmental authority, or if we believe in good faith that such disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, your safety, or the safety of others.

9.5 Business Transfers

If we are involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your data may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you before your data is transferred and becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

10. Device Identifier

The App generates a persistent anonymous device identifier ("device ID") stored in local device storage. This identifier:

  • Is a randomly generated string with no inherent link to your identity
  • Is used to associate local data with sync operations (to distinguish your data from another user's on the same Google account, or from a previous installation)
  • Is not shared with advertisers
  • Is reset if you clear the App's data or reinstall the App

11. Analytics

11.1 Firebase Analytics (Android Only)

The Android version of the App uses Firebase Analytics, provided by Google LLC, to collect anonymised usage data. This helps us understand how users interact with the App and improve the user experience.

What is collected:

  • Screen views and navigation events
  • Feature usage events (e.g., creating a record, adding a wallet, changing settings)
  • Anonymised financial amount ranges (e.g., "0–10", "10–50", "50–100") — exact amounts are never transmitted
  • Onboarding flow events
  • An anonymous app-instance identifier assigned by Firebase to your device installation (a pseudonymous identifier under GDPR)

What is NOT collected:

  • Exact financial amounts, balances, or transaction details
  • Category names you create (custom categories are reported as "custom")
  • Wallet names or profile names
  • Your Google account email or user ID (via analytics)
  • Any data that directly identifies you

11.2 iOS

The iOS version of the App does not collect any analytics data. The analytics implementation on iOS is a no-operation (no-op) stub that sends no data.

11.3 Opt-Out

The App does not currently provide an in-app opt-out for analytics. On Android, you may limit analytics collection by:

  • Disabling "Usage & diagnostics" in your Android device settings
  • Resetting your advertising ID in device settings

11.4 Data Processing

Analytics data is processed by Google LLC as a data processor on our behalf. Data may be stored and processed in the United States and other countries where Google operates, subject to Google's Data Processing Terms.

11.5 Legal Basis

We process analytics data on the basis of legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR). Our legitimate interest is to understand how the App is used in order to improve its features, fix issues, and enhance the user experience. Given the anonymised nature of the data collected and the minimal impact on user privacy, we consider this interest proportionate.

13. Your Rights (GDPR and Applicable Law)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or another jurisdiction with applicable privacy laws, you may have the following rights:

  • Right of access: request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification: request correction of inaccurate personal data
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten"): request deletion of your personal data

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at: support@watch-my-wallet.com

We will respond to requests within 30 days. We may ask you to verify your identity before processing your request.

14. How to Delete Your Data

You can control or delete your data at any time:

  • Delete your account: Settings → Accounts & Profiles → Account actions → Delete account (removes cloud backup, Firebase account, and all local data)
  • Stop sync: Settings → Accounts & Profiles → Account actions → Sign out
  • Revoke Google access: remove App access in your Google Account security settings
  • Delete local data: clear App data in your device's system settings, or uninstall the App

15. California Residents (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • Right to know: you may request information about the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you in the past 12 months.
  • Right to delete: you may request deletion of your personal information, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to opt out of sale: to the extent we share Aggregated Data with third parties in a manner that constitutes a "sale" under CCPA, you have the right to opt out. Aggregated, de-identified data that cannot reasonably be linked back to you is not considered a "sale" of personal information under CCPA. To the extent any sharing qualifies, contact us at support@watch-my-wallet.com to opt out.
  • Right to non-discrimination: we will not discriminate against you for exercising your CCPA rights.

To exercise these rights, contact: support@watch-my-wallet.com
